End Notes (Click to open)

The game is a simple model of how many in Mission are expected to conform in life. We study, our parents encourage us to get a STEM major, and we spend the rest of our lives working. In the game, the speed at which learning takes place is random, as some pople struggle while others coast by in high school. Your satisfaction with your job choice, which was just STEM, also affects how long the "work" portion of the path takes. The loon chase minigame is a reference to a memborable incident in Walden in which the author spends an afternoon chasing a loon. Every time someone completes the entire path without going off track, the game makes it harder for people who play later on to go off the track. This represents Thoreau's conceptions about confirmity.
